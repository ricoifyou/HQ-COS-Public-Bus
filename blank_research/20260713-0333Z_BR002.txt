PROBLEM DEFINITION
A continuity workflow may be unnecessarily slow if it:

- performs transcript, manifest, handoff, pointer, and hash checks sequentially;
- makes the root coordinator repeat reads already completed by helpers;
- emits a long handoff that the user must manually select and copy;
- re-analyzes already-established rules instead of grounding from immutable pointers;
- waits too long before giving visible status;
- treats more agent activity as inherently better regardless of dependency structure;
- shortens the handoff by deleting information instead of separating a short launcher from a complete durable record.

NON-NEGOTIABLE REQUIREMENTS
Any acceptable design must preserve all of the following:

1. Complete durable continuity information remains available.
2. A short launcher is an access envelope, not the complete record.
3. Supplied immutable identity, bytes, LF lines, blob identity, and SHA-256 are verified.
4. A required mismatch or unreadable object causes an explicit integrity stop.
5. Transcript export limitations remain disclosed and are never called absolute complete.
6. Handoffs, manifests, hashes, TXT files, commits, or draft artifacts do not create execution authority.
7. Grounding does not redesign established rules.
8. Missing evidence is reported, not silently reconstructed.
9. Helper summaries do not replace required source verification.
10. The root coordinator remains responsible for reconciliation, contradictions, final status, and user-facing output.
11. This research performs no repository, connector, Project, deployment, secret, paid-service, or external write.
12. Faster operation cannot be achieved by hiding skipped checks.

CANDIDATE DESIGN UNDER REVIEW

A. Two-layer closeout

Layer 1: complete durable package containing, as applicable, chat closeout, handoff, decisions, errors, updates, research, manifest, immutable identities, bytes, LF lines, hashes, known export gaps, authority boundaries, current state, and exact next-work pointer.

Layer 2: one compact directly copyable launcher containing only the minimum safe access envelope. It must not duplicate the full transcript, full handoff, full manifest, detailed receipt, or historical explanation.

B. HOT / COLD continuity split

HOT continuity capsule contains the complete effective state required to resume work: verified current state, active decisions, error-prevention controls, authority, prohibitions, pending items, CURRENT_NEXT_STEP, honesty limitations, and COLD archive inventory.

COLD archive retains complete transcript and evidence records. New chats lazy-fetch COLD material only for disputes, verbatim evidence, or task-specific needs.

The reviewer must determine whether this preserves semantic completeness or creates a dangerous summarized truth source.

C. Parallel closeout candidate

When runtime capacity exists and workstreams are independent:

- Helper A: transcript/export coverage and known-gap classification.
- Helper B: current-state delta, decisions, errors, updates, research, and next action.
- Helper C: short-launcher lint plus adversarial completeness and authority review.
- Root: reconcile contradictions, independently verify, perform the sole authorized write if separately authorized, and produce the final launcher.

Helpers return bounded structured summaries rather than raw logs. Helpers are read-only unless a separate explicit authority says otherwise. Parallel writes are not part of this candidate.

D. Fast grounding candidate

- Immediately report the required traffic-light state and separately state that grounding is in progress.
- Read and verify one immutable HOT continuity capsule.
- Return a compact receipt and immediately continue CURRENT_NEXT_STEP.
- Do not search repositories, list directories, query pull requests, hunt historical artifacts, or silently reconstruct missing pointers.
- Lazy-fetch COLD evidence only when a dispute, verbatim citation, or current task requires it.
- Determine whether a single read is safe, whether two-wave verification is required, and which dependency edges must remain sequential.

E. Dynamic capacity candidate

usable_parallel_helpers = minimum of:
- helpers exposed by the current runtime;

- Give immediate visible status.
